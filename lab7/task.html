Animal History Through Ages

Discover the fascinating journey of animals throughout history

African Lion - The King of Beasts

Lions have roamed the African savannas for over 2 million years. These majestic creatures have been symbols of strength and courage in human culture since ancient times. From the caves of Lascaux to the thrones of medieval kings, lions have captured our imagination and respect.

Historical evidence shows that lions once ranged across most of Africa, parts of Europe, and Asia. They were featured in ancient Egyptian hieroglyphics, Roman colosseums, and Persian art.

African Elephant - Gentle Giants

Elephants have walked the Earth for over 5 million years. These intelligent creatures have played significant roles in human history, from war elephants in ancient Carthage to working animals in Southeast Asian logging operations.

Throughout history, elephants have been revered in many cultures. In Hindu mythology, the elephant-headed god Ganesha is worshipped as the remover of obstacles. African tribes have incorporated elephants into their folklore for centuries.

Dolphins - Intelligent Mariners

Dolphins have inhabited Earth's oceans for over 10 million years. These highly intelligent marine mammals have fascinated humans since ancient times. Greek mythology tells stories of dolphins saving sailors and guiding ships.

Ancient Minoan art from Crete (1600 BCE) depicts dolphins, showing our long-standing relationship with these creatures. Modern research continues to reveal their complex social structures and communication abilities.

Bengal Tiger - Striped Hunters

Tigers have existed for about 2 million years, evolving into the largest of all big cats. These magnificent predators have been deeply woven into Asian culture, from the Chinese zodiac to Hindu mythology.

The tiger has been a symbol of power and protection in many Eastern cultures. In Korea, tigers are seen as guardians; in India, the tiger goddess Durga rides a tiger into battle. Today, they face extinction threats from habitat loss and poaching.
